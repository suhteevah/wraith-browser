## Metering

Usage is metered by **pages loaded** — each call to `browser_navigate` or equivalent MCP tool that loads a new page counts as one page. Interacting with elements, reading from cache, or querying the knowledge graph does not count.

Overages are billed at your plan's per-page rate. You can set hard caps in your dashboard to prevent unexpected charges.

## FAQ

### Can I switch plans mid-cycle?

Yes. Upgrades take effect immediately and are prorated. Downgrades take effect at the start of your next billing cycle.

### Is there an annual discount?

Annual billing is available on Growth and Scale at a 20% discount. Contact sales for enterprise annual agreements.

### What if I need less than 100K pages/month?

Self-host from the [GitHub repo](https://github.com/suhteevah/wraith-browser). Wraith is AGPL-3.0 — free to run on your own infrastructure. The managed plans are for teams that want us to handle operations, monitoring, and support.

### Do self-hosted deployments need a license?

Not if you comply with the AGPL-3.0 license. If you need to embed Wraith in proprietary software without open-source obligations, see our [commercial licensing](/docs/enterprise/licensing) options.

## License enforcement

Wraith Browser is licensed under AGPL-3.0. We actively monitor for compliance and will enforce the license against organizations that use Wraith in proprietary network services without releasing their source code or obtaining a commercial license.

If you are using Wraith in a way that triggers AGPL obligations and have not purchased a commercial license, you are required to release your complete source code under AGPL-3.0. We pursue enforcement through legal counsel when necessary.

If you are unsure whether your use case requires a commercial license, [contact us](/docs/enterprise/contact) — we are happy to clarify.
